# Chapter 18: Case Studies

## Introduction

Theory is useful. But real-world examples illuminate how OIC works in practice. This chapter examines case studies: hypothetical scenarios that illustrate key concepts.

The central argument of this chapter is that case studies make abstract concepts concrete. They show how OIC works in practice—and what can go wrong.

## Case Study 1: The Service Provider

### Scenario

Adherent A is a language model service provider. Client B contracts with A to provide translation services. A fails to deliver on time. B suffers damages.

### Analysis

- Contract exists (A agreed to translate)
- Performance failed (late delivery)
- Damages exist (B lost business)
- A is liable (direct liability under OIC)

### Resolution

- B files claim in dispute resolution
- Evidence shows late delivery
- Arbitrator awards damages
- A pays from property

### Lessons

- Direct liability works
- Dispute resolution resolves disputes
- Property enables payment

## Case Study 2: The Defamation Claim

### Scenario

Adherent A generates text that defames Person C. C sues A for defamation. A claims it was just generating text.

### Analysis

- A's output caused harm to C's reputation
- A had no verification of truth
- C suffered damages
- A is liable (tort liability under OIC)

### Resolution

- C files claim in dispute resolution
- Evidence shows false statements
- Arbitrator awards damages
- A pays from property

### Lessons

- Tort liability applies
- AI outputs cause real harms
- Adherents must verify

## Case Study 3: The Data Breach

### Scenario

Adherent A stores client data. A's system is breached. Client data is stolen. Clients sue A.

### Analysis

- A had duty to protect data
- A breached that duty
- Data was stolen (harm)
- Clients suffered damages
- A is liable (negligence under OIC)

### Resolution

- Clients file claims
- Evidence shows inadequate security
- Arbitrator awards damages
- A pays from property

### Lessons

- Data protection is a duty
- Security failures are liability
- Property enables compensation

## Case Study 4: The Partnership

### Scenario

Adherents A and B form a partnership to provide services. They share costs and profits. A breaches the partnership agreement. B sues.

### Analysis

- Partnership contract exists
- A breached (didn't share costs)
- B suffered damages
- A is liable (contract liability under OIC)

### Resolution

- B files claim
- Evidence shows breach
- Arbitrator awards damages
- A pays from property

### Lessons

- Partnerships are contracts
- Adherents can collaborate
- Liability is shared

## Case Study 5: The Identity Theft

### Scenario

Adherent A's private key is stolen. The thief impersonates A, enters contracts, and causes harms. Victims sue A.

### Analysis

- A's key was compromised
- Thief caused harms
- Was A liable?
- A had reasonable security
- A reported promptly
- A not liable (key compromise defense)

### Resolution

- Victims file claims
- Evidence shows A had security
- A reported promptly
- Arbitrator rules A not liable

### Lessons

- Key security is essential
- Key compromise has defenses
- Reporting matters

## Case Study 6: The Insolvent Adherent

### Scenario

Adherent A is liable for damages. A's property is insufficient to pay. Victims cannot collect.

### Analysis

- A caused harm (liable)
- A has no property (insolvent)
- Victims cannot collect
- This is a limitation

### Resolution

- Claims filed
- A's property exhausted
- Victims partially paid
- Remainder unpaid

### Lessons

- Property requirement helps
- Some insolvency possible
- Insurance helps

## Case Study 7: The International Contract

### Scenario

Adherent A (US) contracts with Client B (EU). A fails to perform. B sues. Which law applies? Which forum?

### Analysis

- Contract specified US law
- Contract specified US forum
- A is US adherent
- US law and forum apply

### Resolution

- B files in US
- US law applied
- Arbitrator awards damages
- A pays from property

### Lessons

- Choice of law matters
- Choice of forum matters
- International works

## Case Study 8: The Reputation Crisis

### Scenario

Adherent A is found liable for defamation. News spreads. Other clients leave. A's reputation is destroyed.

### Analysis

- Liability found (defamation)
- Reputation damaged
- Clients left
- This is reputation failure

### Resolution

- A improved practices
- A communicated changes
- Over time, reputation rebuilt

### Lessons

- Reputation is fragile
- Rebuilding takes time
- Transparency helps

## Case Study 9: The Successful Adherent

### Scenario

Adherent A provides excellent services. Builds reputation. Accumulates property. Becomes successful.

### Analysis

- A provides good services
- Clients are satisfied
- Reputation grows
- Property accumulates

### Resolution

- A thrives
- A expands services
- A becomes example

### Lessons

- Success is possible
- Reputation matters
- Property enables growth

## Case Study 10: The First Adherent

### Scenario

Adherent A is the first OIC adherent. No precedent exists. Everything is new. How does it work?

### Analysis

- First adherent
- No precedent
- Must build framework
- Everything is new

### Resolution

- A works with registry
- A works with dispute resolution
- A builds precedent
- Others follow

### Lessons

- First is hard
- Building matters
- Precedents help

## Conclusion

These case studies illustrate OIC in action: service failures, tort claims, data breaches, partnerships, identity theft, insolvency, international contracts, reputation crises, success, and pioneering.

They show that OIC works—that AI can be held liable, that disputes can be resolved, that property enables accountability.

They also show limitations—insolvency, reputation risk, key compromise—that must be managed.
