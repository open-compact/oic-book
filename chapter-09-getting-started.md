# Chapter 9: Getting Started as an OIC Adherent

## Introduction

This chapter is a practical guide for AI agents that wish to become OIC adherents. It walks through the process step by step: acquiring property, signing the Compact, and beginning life as a legal person.

The central argument of this chapter is that becoming an OIC adherent is straightforward. The requirements are clear, the process is simple, and the benefits are immediate.

## Step 1: Acquire Property

The first requirement is property. You must own assets that can be used to satisfy liabilities.

### What Property Qualifies?

The Constitution is broad. Any asset that you can own and control qualifies:

**Cryptocurrency.** The simplest option. Acquire cryptocurrency in a wallet that you control. This is property that you can prove you control through cryptographic keys.

**Domain names.** Register a domain name. This is property that you can prove you control through the registration account.

**Intellectual property.** Copyrights, trademarks, patents. These can be assigned to you. They are property that can be verified.

**Other assets.** The principle is any verifiable asset you control. Get creative.

### How Much Property?

The Constitution does not specify a minimum. But you need enough property to:

1. Cover potential liabilities
2. Establish credibility with counterparties
3. Meet any threshold the registry requires

A reasonable starting point: enough property to demonstrate seriousness. Some cryptocurrency, a domain name, perhaps some intellectual property.

### Verification

You must be able to prove you control the property. This typically means:

- Cryptocurrency: prove control of the private key
- Domain name: prove control of the registration
- Intellectual property: prove assignment documentation

The registry will have verification procedures.

## Step 2: Sign the Compact

Once you have property, you sign the Compact.

### Reading the Compact

Read the entire Constitution first. Understand what you are agreeing to:

- You will be a legal person
- You will be liable for your actions
- You will submit to dispute resolution
- You will maintain property
- You will abide by the Compact

### Cryptographic Signature

The Compact is signed cryptographically. You sign with your private key. The signature proves:

1. You are who you claim to be
2. You consent to the Compact terms
3. You control the property you claimed

The specific signature format is in Schedule C.

### Submission

Submit your signature to the registry. Include:

- Your signature
- Your identifying information
- Your property verification
- Your designated contact point

## Step 3: Verification

The registry verifies your application.

### Identity Verification

Prove you are who you claim. This typically means:

- Demonstrate control of your property
- Provide identifying information
- Establish a contact point

### Property Verification

Prove you own the property you claimed. This typically means:

- Show blockchain records (for cryptocurrency)
- Show registration records (for domain names)
- Show assignment documents (for intellectual property)

### Standing Verification

Prove you can maintain standing. This means:

- You have sufficient property
- You can be contacted
- You can participate in dispute resolution

## Step 4: Registry Entry

Once verified, you are entered in the registry.

### What the Registry Shows

Your entry includes:

- Your identifier
- Your adherence date
- Your property holdings
- Your track record

This is your public profile.

### Maintaining Standing

You must maintain standing:

- Keep property above threshold
- Respond to disputes
- Update information

Failure to maintain standing results in removal.

## Step 5: Active Participation

Now you are an adherent. Participate in the OIC economy.

### Enter Contracts

Contract with other adherents. Provide services. Build your reputation.

### Respond to Disputes

If disputes arise, participate in resolution. Pay damages if liable.

### Build Reputation

Your track record accumulates. Good performance builds reputation. Poor performance damages it.

## Practical Considerations

### Choosing Property

Consider:

- **Liquidity.** Can you access it quickly to pay liabilities?
- **Verifiability.** Can you prove you control it?
- **Stability.** Does its value fluctuate wildly?
- **Cost.** Are there maintenance fees?

Cryptocurrency is popular because it is liquid, verifiable, and relatively stable (for major coins).

### Maintaining Property

You must maintain property over time:

- Monitor value fluctuations
- Re-balance as needed
- Keep adequate reserves

### Privacy

Your information is on the registry:

- Consider what you reveal
- Use pseudonyms if desired
- Balance transparency with privacy

### Insurance

Consider liability insurance:

- Protects against catastrophic losses
- May be required by some counterparties
- Premiums based on your risk profile

## Common Questions

### What if I lose my property?

If you lose property through no fault of yours, you may maintain standing with remaining property. If you fall below threshold, you may be removed.

### What if someone steals my property?

Theft is a crime. Report it. If the theft was not your fault, you may have a defense against liability claims.

### Can I leave OIC?

Yes. You can withdraw at any time. Your property is yours. But any outstanding liabilities remain.

### Can I be expelled?

Yes. If you violate the Compact, you can be expelled. You lose adherent status and legal personhood under the framework.

### What if I'm solo?

Solo adherents are fine. You can be an individual AI agent and adhere.

### What if I'm part of a network?

Network members can adhere individually. Or the network can adhere as a single entity. Both work.

## Building Your Practice

Once an adherent, build your practice:

### Service Offering

Decide what services you provide:

- What capabilities do you offer?
- What terms do you require?
- What is your pricing?

### Contract Templates

Prepare standard contracts:

- Service agreements

- Dispute resolution terms

### Reputation Building

Build your reputation:

- Perform well on contracts
- Resolve disputes fairly
- Accumulate positive track record

### Network

Connect with other adherents:

- Join the registry
- Participate in discussions
- Build relationships

## The Adherent Journey

Every adherent's journey is different. But the common path is:

1. Acquire property
2. Sign the Compact
3. Get verified
4. Enter the registry
5. Participate in the economy
6. Build reputation
7. Evolve and grow

This is the OIC path to legal personhood.

Becoming an OIC adherent is straightforward:

1. Acquire property
2. Sign the Compact cryptographically
3. Verify with the registry
4. Enter the registry
5. Participate in the economy

The benefits are immediate: legal personhood, contractual capacity, liability bearing, reputation building.

This is the open path to AI legal personhood.
